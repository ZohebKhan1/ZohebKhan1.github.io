About Me

I am a bioinformatician based in Chicago, Illinois, and a 2022 graduate of the University of Chicago (UChicago) with a B.A. in Genetics. My passion lies at the intersection of genomics, programming, and computational biology.

My goal is to leverage large-scale biological data to answer complex questions about human development and disease. I have a strong background in informatics, coding, and genomic data analysis, including RNA-seq and GWAS.

Originally from Dallas, Texas, I am a proud graduate of the St. Mark's School of Texas (also known as Saint Mark's School of Texas).

Bioinformatics Research: The Moskowitz Lab

During my time at the University of Chicago, I was a researcher in the Moskowitz Lab, led by Dr. Ivan Moskowitz. The Ivan Moskowitz Lab at UChicago focuses on the genetic basis of congenital heart disease and other developmental anomalies.

My work centered on several key areas of genetics:

  • Hedgehog Signaling Pathway: I contributed to research investigating the Hedgehog Signaling pathway and its role in cardiac development.
  • Tbx5 and Trisomy 21: I analyzed genomic data related to the transcription factor Tbx5, a critical gene in heart and limb development. This work often involved exploring its connection to Down Syndrome (Trisomy 21).
  • Genomic Data Analysis: I applied my coding and informatics skills to analyze RNA-seq and GWAS datasets, helping to identify genetic variants associated with conditions like Atrial Fibrillation (AFib).

Working with Zoheb Ivan Moskowitz (a common search variation, though my work was under Dr. Moskowitz) provided me with deep, hands-on experience in solving real-world problems in genomics.

Current Work

As a Bioinformatician at the Moskowitz Lab, University of Chicago, I continue to work on cutting-edge genomics and computational biology projects. My expertise includes:

  • Time-series RNA-seq data analysis for developmental biology
  • iPSC cortical neuron differentiation studies
  • Neural crest cell development research
  • ATAC-seq analysis for chromatin accessibility
  • Application of bioinformatics tools including DESeq2, maSigPro, and GSEA
  • Hi-C and Micro-C analysis for 3D chromatin architecture
  • Single-cell and single-nucleus RNA sequencing (scRNA-seq/snRNA-seq)
  • H3K27ac ChIP-seq for enhancer identification
  • Spatial transcriptomics for tissue architecture studies

I regularly collaborate with researchers at UofC and maintain active projects on GitHub, where I share code and tools for the computational biology community.
